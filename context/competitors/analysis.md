## Title Patterns

### Formula 1: "The [Adjective] [Noun] of [Entity]" (Definite Article + Modifier)

**Examples:**
- The Dark Side Of The Silk Road (13.2M)

- The CIA's Cafeteria Drama (351K)

**Average views:** 3.0M | **Sample size:** 10 | **Performance: HIGH**

This is the dominant formula (10 of 40 titles). "The Dark Side Of..." is a proven sub-pattern (2 uses, both above median). Adding "Mysterious", "Bizarre", or "Cryptic" as the adjective signals mystery genre. Titles with a specific entity (Flight 980, Silk Road) outperform vague ones (Antarctica, Lake Mead).

**Emotional hook:** Revelation -- promises hidden knowledge about a known entity.

### Key Takeaways for Our Channel

1. **Use Formula 1 or 3 as primary structure** -- "The [Adjective] [Noun] of [Entity]" is the workhorse; extraordinary claims have the highest ceiling.
2. **Add "An Unsolved Mystery" subtitle selectively** -- only when the story genuinely has no resolution. Overuse dilutes the signal.
3. **Anchor superlatives to locations** -- "Mexico's Most..." outperforms generic superlatives. Aligns with our obscure international focus.
4. **Avoid minimalist titles** without established brand recognition.
5. **Specificity wins** -- titles naming a concrete entity (Flight 980, Silk Road, Room 322) outperform abstract ones (Antarctica, Lake Mead) within the same formula.
